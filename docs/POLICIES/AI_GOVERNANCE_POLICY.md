AI GOVERNANCE POLICY

LEGACY AI

Institutional Governance Platform

---

Policy Purpose

This policy establishes the governance requirements governing the development, deployment, operation, review, and oversight of artificial intelligence systems within the Legacy AI environment.

The objective of this policy is to preserve accountability, maintain record integrity, support institutional continuity, and ensure that artificial intelligence remains subject to human authority and documented governance controls.


Policy Statement

Artificial intelligence shall operate as a governed support system.

Artificial intelligence shall not operate as an autonomous authority.

All material outputs remain subject to human review, verification, and approval.

Governance must precede automation.


Scope

This policy applies to:

- platform administrators
- developers
- governance reviewers
- institutional users
- operational personnel
- third-party integrations

This policy governs all AI-assisted activities conducted through the Legacy AI platform.


Governance Principles

Legacy AI shall operate according to the following principles:

- Governance Precedes Automation
- Verification Precedes Recognition
- Record Integrity Governs Accountability
- Capacity Determines Consequence
- Human Review Remains Final
- Institutional Continuity Requires Preservation
- Authority Must Remain Traceable


Human Oversight Requirements

Human oversight remains mandatory for:

- governance decisions
- policy implementation
- operational approvals
- external communications
- institutional publications
- system deployment actions

No AI-generated output shall be considered self-authorizing.


Verification Standards

All significant system actions shall support:

- attribution
- traceability
- review identification
- timestamp preservation
- audit history retention

Verification status classifications include:

- Draft
- Reviewed
- Approved
- Verified
- Archived


Record Preservation Requirements

Legacy AI shall preserve records supporting:

- governance activities
- policy modifications
- operational decisions
- review actions
- verification events
- continuity requirements

Records should remain attributable and recoverable.


Accountability Standards

All actions should identify:

- responsible actor
- operational capacity
- authority source
- action performed
- review status

Accountability requires traceability.


Data Governance

The platform shall support:

- appropriate access controls
- data classification
- retention management
- record preservation
- audit support

Sensitive information shall be handled according to applicable legal, contractual, and organizational requirements.


Prohibited Uses

Legacy AI shall not be used to:

- bypass governance controls
- misrepresent approval status
- falsify records
- conceal accountability
- automate institutional authority without oversight
- remove required human review processes


Audit and Review

Governance controls shall be reviewed periodically to ensure:

- policy compliance
- operational effectiveness
- record integrity
- accountability preservation
- continuity protection


Enforcement

Failure to comply with this policy may result in:

- review escalation
- access restrictions
- administrative intervention
- governance remediation measures
